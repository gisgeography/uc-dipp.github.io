I'm Alex Warofka a first-year MPP student at the Harris School of Public Policy and research associate at the UCLA California Center for Population Research. I recently completed a Fulbright Fellowship in Japan, studying the economics and human rights impacts of Japan’s Industrial Trainee and Technical Internship Programme.

I completed my undergraduate degree in economics in the US at Case Western Reserve University in Cleveland, Ohio with concentrations in finance and Japanese language. I have also studied at Peking University, Kyoto University, and the London School of Economics. I continue to assist the Department of Economics at CWRU on several projects, including an ongoing effort to map the US automotive supply chain.

When not busy with my research pursuits, I enjoy travelling, photography, and hiking. I’ve lived in five countries and explored three dozen more. I’m equally at home relaxing in a New York high-rise or working with NGOs in the remote villages of rural Bangladesh. I’m particularly fond of embarking on ill-advised adventures, such as the Mongol Rally and excursions to members of the Axis of Evil.

I am proficient in English, Japanese, and conversational Mandarin.
